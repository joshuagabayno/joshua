Holistic Care for Mental Wellbeing

In a world that often prioritizes physical health, mental wellbeing frequently gets overlooked. This isn't just about the absence of illness; it's about cultivating a thriving mind, capable of resilience, joy, and connection. This advocacy aims to raise awareness about mental health, provide vital information, and encourage healthy behaviors that foster a mentally healthy life for everyone.We believe that open conversations, accessible resources, and supportive communities are key to breaking down the stigma surrounding mental health challenges.

About Us

We are a collective of individuals passionate about promoting mental wellness in Bangad, Cabanatuan City. Our team includes mental health professionals, advocates, and individuals with lived experience. We are united by a shared belief that everyone deserves access to support and understanding when facing mental health challenges. We are committed to providing accurate information, fostering empathy, and building a supportive community where open dialogue is encouraged and help is readily available.

Importance

Mental health is integral to overall wellbeing. It impacts every aspect of our lives, from our relationships and work performance to our physical health and overall happiness. A healthy mind allows us to navigate life's challenges with resilience, experience joy and fulfillment, and build meaningful connections with others. Neglecting mental health can lead to various challenges, including anxiety, depression, stress, and burnout. Prioritizing mental wellbeing is not selfish; it's essential for leading a balanced and fulfilling life. Early intervention and ongoing support are crucial for preventing serious mental health issues and promoting long-term wellbeing.

Cases in the Philippines

'Mental health crisis a rising epidemic in PH' – experts

The Philippines Mental Health Association (PMHA) declared a rising epidemic of mental health crisis in the Philippines, citing a sharp increase in concerns after the Covid-19 pandemic lockdowns. They highlighted limited access to mental health services due to stigma and scarce resources, with less than one mental health worker per 100,000 Filipinos. The PMHA called for a whole-of-society approach and greater accessibility to diagnosis, treatment, and care. This situation underscores the urgent need for stronger and more localized mental health responses in the country.

Rising mental disorder cases among Filipino seafarers

A study by the National Maritime Polytechnic (NMP) revealed a rise in mental disorders among Filipino seafarers, with cases increasing from 23 in 2018 to 64 in 2022. Anxiety and depressive disorders were most common, and suicide cases rose from one in 2018 to nine in 2022. The pandemic exacerbated mental health issues, with isolation and extended contracts contributing to increased stress. Seafarers aged 30 to 39 were most affected by these issues.

Mental Health on the Move in the Philippines

The COVID-19 pandemic increased unmet mental health needs in the Philippines, with the DOH estimating at least 3.6 million Filipinos facing mental health issues. Government-mandated lockdowns and social isolation exacerbated existing challenges, impacting mental health directly. A DOH study found that one in three COVID-19 patients developed a mental health condition within six months. The Lusog-Isip app, designed to improve mental health, provides self-help resources and targeted support to users.
